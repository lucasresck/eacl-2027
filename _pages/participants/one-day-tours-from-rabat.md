<h2>Fez</h2>

<p>Organized by: Amwaj Travel</p>

<p>Fez, also spelled Fes, is located in the interior of Morocco and approximately 300 km (200 miles) from its north coast, being the second most touristic city in the country, just below Marrakech. It’s also the oldest of the imperial cities and current religious capital of the country, with a population of approximately two million inhabitants.</p>

<h3>The Medina (Fez el-Bali):</h3>
<p>Explore the largest medina in the world, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, entering through the famous blue gate, Bab Bou Jeloud.</p>

<h3>The Chouara Tanneries:</h3>
<p>Observe the colorful dye vats, a traditional and unmissable spectacle.</p>

<h3>The Madrasas (Koranic Schools):</h3>
<p>Visit the Bou Inania Madrasa and the Attarine Madrasa for their refined Marinid architecture.</p>

<h3>The Andalusian Quarter and Al Quaraouiyine Mosque:</h3>
<p>Discover the spiritual center and the oldest university in the world.</p>

<h3>The Royal Palace (Gates):</h3>
<p>Admire the impressive architecture of the palace gates in the Fez el-Jdid district.</p>

<h3>Fez Souks:</h3>
<p>Stroll through the coppersmiths' souk, the henna souk, and the Kisaria Al Kifah.</p>

<p>Transportation and a guide will be at your disposal to explore the city's main tourist attractions.</p>

<h2>Casablanca</h2>

<p>Organized by: Amwaj Travel</p>

<p>Departure in the morning to Casablanca to discover its most famous tourist and historical landmarks in a pleasant and well-organized atmosphere.</p>

<h3>First stop: Hassan II Mosque</h3>
<p>Visit one of the largest mosques in the world, with its stunning architecture and unique location overlooking the Atlantic Ocean. Part of the mosque is built over the ocean, featuring a retractable roof that opens for special occasions. It boasts exquisite traditional Moroccan decorations, including zellige tilework, wood carvings, and stucco work.</p>

<p>An opportunity to take amazing photos and appreciate the splendor of Moroccan architecture.</p>

<h3>Second stop / Ain Diab Corniche</h3>
<p>A pleasant stroll along the Corniche with free time to relax or enjoy a coffee with a beautiful sea view.</p>
<p>Lunch break and free time at one of the restaurants in Ain Diab or the city center to sample Moroccan or modern dishes of your choice.</p>

<h3>Third stop / Mohammed V Square</h3>
<p>A tour of the administrative square, reflecting the distinctive architectural style of Casablanca.</p>

<h3>Fourth stop / Casablanca Old City</h3>
<p>A leisurely walk through the traditional alleyways and free time to purchase souvenirs.</p>

<h3>End of the day with a visit to / Casablanca Marina</h3>
<p>A modern atmosphere and a beautiful view to end the day with a memorable group photo.</p>
