<h1>Half Day Guided Sightseeing Tour of Rabat</h1>

<p>
A typical half-day (3–4 hour) city tour of Rabat focuses on its top UNESCO World Heritage sites, combining ancient Roman ruins, medieval architecture, and the bustling, blue-and-white Kasbah of the Oudayas.
Tours usually start with the <strong>Chellah necropolis</strong>, followed by the <strong>Hassan Tower</strong> and <strong>Mausoleum of Mohammed V</strong>, and finish with a walk through the <strong>Oudayas Kasbah</strong> and the <strong>Old Medina</strong>.
</p>

<h2>Key stops on the tour</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Chellah Necropolis:</strong> Ancient Roman ruins and Merinid-era tombs.</li>
  <li><strong>Hassan Tower &amp; Mausoleum of Mohammed V:</strong> The 12th-century unfinished minaret and the ornate, modern royal tomb.</li>
  <li><strong>Kasbah of the Oudayas:</strong> A fortified 12th-century fortress, offering stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean and the Bou Regreg River.</li>
  <li><strong>Andalusian Gardens:</strong> Located inside the Kasbah for a quick, tranquil walk.</li>
  <li><strong>Old Medina:</strong> A walk through the traditional, bustling market streets.</li>
  <li><strong>Royal Palace (Exterior):</strong> A quick stop for photos of the impressive Mechouar.</li>
</ul>

<p>
The tour costs $15 to be paid on site, and includes transportation and an English-speaking guide.<br>

<strong>Note:</strong> The price does <strong>not</strong> include museum entrance fees, other attractions, or meals.
